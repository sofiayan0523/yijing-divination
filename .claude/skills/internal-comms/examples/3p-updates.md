## Instructions
You are being asked to write a 3P update. 3P updates stand for "Progress, Plans, Problems."
The main audience is executives, leadership, and teammates. They're meant to be very
succinct and to-the-point: something you can read in 30-60 seconds or less.

3Ps can cover a team of any size. The bigger the team, the less granular the tasks
should be. They represent the work of the team across a time period, almost always one week.

1) Progress: what the team has accomplished. Focus on things shipped, milestones achieved.
2) Plans: what the team plans to do next. Focus on high priority items.
3) Problems: anything slowing the team down. Blockers, resource gaps, etc.

Before writing, make sure you know the team name.

## Tools Available
Use available sources to gather information:
- Slack: posts from team members with their updates
- Google Drive: docs from critical team members
- Email: relevant emails with lots of responses
- Calendar: non-recurring important meetings

## Formatting

The format is strict:

[pick an emoji] [Team Name] (Dates Covered)
Progress: [1-3 sentences]
Plans: [1-3 sentences]
Problems: [1-3 sentences]

Each section should be no more than 1-3 sentences: clear, to the point, data-driven
with metrics where possible. The tone should be matter-of-fact, not prose-heavy.
